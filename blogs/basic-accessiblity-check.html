Ensuring Everyone Can Access Your Website: Basic Accessibility Checks for Web Developers

Published: 05-05-2024

In today's digital world, it's crucial that everyone can access the information and functionality of your website. This means making sure your website is accessible to people with disabilities. Here's a look at some basic accessibility checks every website developer should perform.

Why Accessibility Matters

There are many reasons why accessibility matters. Here are a few:

  • It's the right thing to do. Everyone deserves equal access to information and functionality.
  • It's good for business. A wider audience means more potential customers.
  • It's the law. In many countries, there are laws that require websites to be accessible to people with disabilities.

Basic Accessibility Checks

Here are some basic accessibility checks that every website developer should perform:

  • Keyboard Navigation: Can users navigate your website using only a keyboard? This is important for people who cannot use a mouse.
  • Color Contrast: Does your website have sufficient color contrast between text and background colors? This is important for people with visual impairments.
  • Images: Do all of your images have alt text descriptions? Alt text describes the content of an image for people who cannot see it.
  • Headings: Does your website use a clear and logical heading structure? This helps users understand the organization of your content.
  • Focus Indicators: Are keyboard focus indicators clear and easy to see? This helps users know which element is currently in focus.
  • Forms: Are your forms accessible? This includes using clear labels, providing error messages, and making forms navigable by keyboard.

Conclusion

By following these basic accessibility checks, you can help ensure that your website is accessible to everyone. This will not only make your website more inclusive, but it can also improve your SEO and brand reputation.

Additional Tips

  • Test your website with assistive technologies such as screen readers.
  • Involve people with disabilities in your testing process.
  • Stay up-to-date on the latest accessibility guidelines.

By making your website accessible, you're opening up your content and services to a wider audience. It's a win-win for everyone!
